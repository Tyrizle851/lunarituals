How to Use Affirmations Effectively

By Luna Rituals Team • 7 min read • Updated Oct 2025

Affirmations have been around for decades, but many people try them once and give up, thinking they "don't work." The truth is, affirmations are a powerful tool when used correctly.

Based on cognitive behavioral therapy and neuroscience research, here's how to make affirmations actually stick and transform your mindset.

The Science Behind Affirmations

Our brains have something called a "negativity bias" - we're wired to focus on threats and problems. This helped our ancestors survive, but in modern life, it often leads to anxiety and self-doubt.

Affirmations work by creating new neural pathways. When you repeat positive statements consistently, you're literally rewiring your brain to focus on what's going well.

Common Mistakes People Make

  • Using the wrong tense: "I will be confident" vs "I am confident"
  • Being too vague: "I'm good" vs "I am a skilled writer who creates valuable content"
  • Not believing the words: If you don't believe it, your brain rejects it
  • Saying them once: Consistency is key - repetition builds the neural pathway

The Right Way to Practice

1. Make Them Specific and Personal

Instead of "I am successful," try "I am a creative entrepreneur who builds meaningful products that help people."

2. Use Present Tense

Your brain responds better to statements that feel true now, not in the future.

3. Include Emotion and Sensory Details

"I feel confident and capable" is more powerful than just "I am confident."

4. Practice Consistently

Start with 2-3 minutes daily. Morning works well because it sets your mindset for the day.

5. Say Them with Conviction

Look in the mirror, stand tall, and speak with confidence. Your body language reinforces the message.

Advanced Techniques

Future Self Visualization

Write affirmations as if you're giving advice to your future self: "You are capable, you are worthy, you are exactly where you need to be."

Gratitude + Affirmation Combo

Start with what you're grateful for, then move into affirmations. This creates a positive emotional state that makes affirmations more effective.

Record and Listen

Record yourself saying affirmations in a calm, confident voice. Listen during your commute or while doing chores.
